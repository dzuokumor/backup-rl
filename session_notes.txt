KEY OBSERVATIONS
-----------------

1. PPO agent's strategy: often deliberately disconnects Egbin's two lines early,
   isolating it from the rest of the grid. This trips 3 smaller generators but
   Egbin (3.32 pu) can carry all load alone. The agent learned this is safer than
   managing cascading failures across the full grid.

2. With optimized env (auto-recovery), faults are temporary — lines reconnect after
   20 steps. This prevents the accumulation of disconnected lines that causes
   terminal conditions.

3. Generator restart logic is critical for demo — without it, once 3 generators
   trip at step ~2-3, the city goes permanently dark even though Egbin serves 100%.

4. Demand vs generation imbalance: base demand ~2.67 pu but initial generation only
   ~2.1 pu. Egbin alone can serve it (max 3.32 pu). The smaller generators
   contribute ~0.6 pu combined.

5. The -10.0 cascade penalty dominates the reward function. The agent's primary
   learned behavior is cascade avoidance above all else.

6. Random agent survives ~9 steps average. Do-nothing survives ~24 steps. PPO
   routinely survives 1000 steps — orders of magnitude improvement.
